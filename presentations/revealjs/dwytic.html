Time to pay attention

The Lie

Does going to school pay off? Most people think so. Currently almost 90 percent of young adults graduate from high school and about 60 percent of high school seniors continue on to college the following year. People decide to go to college for many reasons. One of the most compelling is the expectation of future economic success based on educational attainment.
  • Introduction to a 2002 report from the U.S. Census Bureau titled “The Big Payoff: Educational Attainment and Synthetic Estimates of Work-Life Earnings”
  • specifically avoid saying “A college degree results in greater earnings,” but this is the obvious conclusion
  • Media outlets and college marketing departments simply can’t stop themselves

Correlation vs Causation

  • Not obvious because the numbers show that people who go to college earn more
  • Fundamental less on statistics

Useless Correlation

  • Raw data can suggest a relationship but cannot imply the direction of the relationship or even establish clearly if there is a relationship all
  • Chart was produced using data from a similar U.S. government agency covering the same time period

Useful correlation

“We find that students who attended more selective colleges earned about the same as students of seemingly comparable ability who attended less selective schools”
- Stacy Berg Dale and Alan B. Krueger

  • Being accepted to an elite program is just as good as matriculating
  • 100 years ago, college was accessible only to the privileged. Anointed leaders
  • Today is different. What is going wrong for so many people?

Chasing Correlations


“Look... me and the McDonald's people got this little misunderstanding. See, they're McDonald's... I'm McDowell's. They got the Golden Arches, mine is the Golden Arcs. They got the Big Mac, I got the Big Mick.”
  • Legend of how McDonald's and Burger King identify new franchise locations
  • McDonald's is examining causal relationships. BK is chasing correlations
  • Many employers behave like BK, treating colleges like McDonald's

Remember the Bear!

What is the point of college in the first place?
What makes graduates more attractive to employers?
What is it that the college admissions offices are identifying?
  • Problem of people who focus too much on "looking" like a promising candidate rather than being one
  • What does it even mean to run from the bear?

Educated People


“I'm an educated man, but I'm afraid I can't speak intelligently about the travel habits of William Santiago.”
  • Colleges were developed as an alternative to trade schools or vocational programs.
  • What has changed is how colleges are marketed to students, parents and employers.
  • Marketing messages aren’t consistent with the messages coming from other functions of the college
  • Previous generations had more clarity about college and used the term “educated man” to connote this meaning

Educated People

“An educated man is one who can entertain a new idea, entertain another person and entertain himself.”
- Sydney Wood

  • The point of college is exactly this. To first understand what it means to be an educated person, and then to become that person.

Why Employers Hire


“Well, the world needs ditch diggers, too.”
  1. The candidate has skills that are valued by the organization
  2. The candidate demonstrates raw abilities that will lead to the development of valuable skills
  3. The candidate is willing to do things that people who meet the first two criteria do not want to do

Valuable Skills


“You keep using that word, I do not think it means what you think it means”
  • The first group, possessing valuable skills, has nothing to worry about.
  • The second group is where most college graduates envision themselves.
  • Most graduates aspire to move from the second group to the first group ASAP.
  • Third group is eaten by the bear.

Internships

  • Focus on Internships, but same causality trap applies.
  • Too much of a focus pushes candidates into the first group at the expense of the second.
  • In this path, graduation no longer triggers "opportunity", it actually ends it.
  • You don't even have four years to put it all together

It's Not Too Late


“So you're telling me there's a chance”
  • This will scare some people, and it changes the question.
  • When should I start running?
  • Others already started, is it too late?

What Colleges Are Looking For

Communication
Achievement
Leadership
  • Identify those who will become the educated people worthy of investment
  • Identify the members of the second group
  • Each one these abilities has a multiplicative effect on your professional skills

What Colleges Ask For

Academic record
Written Essays
Extracurriculars
An in-person interview
  • Face value is hard to apply to the workforce. Markers for the key skills makes more sense.
  • Looking at the admissions process as a cynical number-crunching exercise acknowledges only the correlation.
  • The application is an opportunity to demonstrate the development of these raw skills
  • Chasing correlations puts you in the first group in the best case

The Second Group

  • Stasis is impossible. Skills are either improving or declining. "Valuable" is a moving target
  • Realizing the responsibility for maintenance implies responsibility for initial acquisition.
  • Professional skills development is most effective when an individual is personally committed to the pursuit.
  • The people that are the most successful in today’s workplace never leave the second group.

“Experience is what you get when you didn't get what you wanted.”
- Randy Pausch

  • False conundrum “How am I supposed to get experience if someone won’t give me a job.”

More Experience

"Good judgment comes from experience, and a lot of that comes from bad judgment."
- Will Rodgers or Jim Horning or Mulla Nasreddin or ...

  • “Experience” in this case isn’t solely relate to professional experience, but rather all experience.
  • Person has neither skills nor promise (first and second groups)
  • Person has been eaten by the bear

Self-Awareness

“If you know your enemies and know yourself, you will not be imperiled in a hundred battles... if you do not know your enemies nor yourself, you will be imperiled in every single battle.”
- Sun Tzu

  • Running from the bear vs. running from the bear efficiently
  • Variance for each individual. Self-awareness is key

Limitations


“Freddy, what I am saying is: know your limitations. You are a moron.”
  • Knowledge of one’s self ends up being of critical importance in many aspects of life.
  • Impossible to learn this without gaining experience.
  • Don't wait for someone to tell you. They could be wrong!
  • Find out for yourself!

Surpassing Limitations

  • Important to do as a student
  • People tend to get more conservative as they age.
  • Must surpass limits to find them.
  • This is an exhausting and destructive process.

Leadership

“Leadership is getting someone to do what they don't want to do, to achieve what they want to achieve.”
- Tom Landry

  • Hard to do. Almost impossible to do by yourself.
  • Find someone who is able and willing to lead them past their limits.
  • Commit to the process.

Achievement


“No. Try not. Do... or do not. There is no try.”
  • Finding limits in valueless activities is not constructive
  • Find limits such that those activities result in achievement
  • Waiting to do something “until you are ready” is a dangerous delay tactic.
  • Remove the qualifiers. Just be an XXX. Even a bad one.

Problems and Solutions

  • Wanting focuses on the glory of the result.
  • Doing exposes the challenge.
  • This exposure to challenge is the catalyst for true learning.
  • It is difficult to understand and appreciate solutions without understanding and appreciating the corresponding problems.

Value Extraction


“Grab a brew. Don't cost nothin'.”
  • When a student agrees to go to a college, they commit to pay
  • Only gets worse if you quit.
  • Students need to make a more deliberate effort to avail themselves to the opportunities that they have already paid for.
  • Colleges are built to develop those core skills. Take advantage of the opportunity.

Oh, so, you're saying I should hire you because you don't work very hard when the work is boring? Well, there's boring stuff in programming, too. Every job has its boring moments. And I don't want to hire people that only want to do the fun stuff.
- Joel Spolsky, founder Fog Creek Software

  • Covered a lot of ground without talking about academics.
  • Just as important as the other elements

Fixed vs. Growth Mindset

“In a fixed mindset, people believe their basic qualities, like their intelligence or talent, are simply fixed traits. They spend their time documenting their intelligence or talent instead of developing them. They also believe that talent alone creates success—without effort. They’re wrong.”
- Carol S. Dweck, author of Mindset

  • GPA is an opportunity for personal achievement.
  • Do not accept that you have limitations that cannot be surpassed.
  • Often a function of time committed and personal interest.
  • Find an adviser or mentor or rival to help.

Academic Projects


"This? This is ice. This is what happens to water when it gets too cold."
  • There are broad opportunities for academic projects outside of the classroom.
  • Can help students find the context they need to understand the lessons from the classroom.
  • More effective than internships at mastering the material.
